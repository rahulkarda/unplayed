How to use this

Playing & Exploring

♟Click a piece to select it. Legal moves appear as dots. Click a dot to move.
⟵Back button undoes the last move. Start jumps to the beginning. Click any move in the history to jump there.
🎲Random Move plays a legal move at random — great for diving deep into unknown territory fast.
♙→♛When a pawn promotes, a picker appears — choose Queen, Rook, Bishop, or Knight.

Evaluation

⚖The bar and score show material + position balance. Positive = white advantage, negative = black. It's a simple estimate, not an engine.

Territory

🗺Opening territory (moves 1–5): well-trodden ground, millions of games here.
🌲Still in theory (moves 5–10): known to opening specialists, rare to most players.
🔭Possibly uncharted (moves 10–20): you may be the first person to reach this exact position.
✦Almost certainly uncharted (move 20+): this position has almost certainly never been played by a human.

Drop a Pin

📍Found something interesting? Name it and pin it. Your atlas saves to your browser and persists across sessions. Use tags to categorise what you find.

Quick Explore

⚡Jump to famous opening positions: King's Indian, Sicilian (sharp openings), Endgame (king + pawn structure), Chaos (a position with castling rights and active pieces).

FEN

📋FEN is the standard notation for any chess position. Click it to copy — paste into Lichess, Chess.com, or any engine to analyse further.
